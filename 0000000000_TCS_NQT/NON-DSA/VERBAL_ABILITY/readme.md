TCS NQT Verbal Ability tests reading speed, grammar fundamentals, and contextual vocabulary.
In the actual exam, Verbal is often used as a massive time-drainer. Long paragraphs
and complex sentence structures are intentionally introduced to disrupt your pacing.

THE CORE STRATEGY: You must categorize questions instantly. Grammar rules should take
under 30 seconds, leaving extra time for Reading Comprehension and Cloze Tests.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
🟥 TIER 1: CORE GRAMMAR & MAXIMUM WEIGHTAGE (The 30-Second ROI Boosters)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
These topics form the bedrock of the Verbal section. They appear heavily in Error Spotting
and Sentence Correction. If you know the rules, you can clear these in 30-40 seconds.

1. Subject-Verb Agreement
   - Subtopics: Singular/Plural verbs, collective nouns, proximity rules
   - Weightage: Very High (3-4 Questions)
   - Solvability in 60 Sec: Easy (30 - 45 seconds)
   - TCS Internal Insight / Trap to Avoid: TCS loves placing long clauses between the
     subject and the verb to confuse you. Always locate the true subject first.

2. Prepositions & Conjunctions
   - Subtopics: Fixed prepositions, correlative conjunctions (neither...nor, not only...but also)
   - Weightage: High (2-3 Questions)
   - Solvability in 60 Sec: Easy (25 - 40 seconds)
   - The Shortcut: Memorize fixed prepositions (e.g., 'abstain from', 'comply with').
     Look for structural mismatches in parallel structures immediately.

3. Tenses & Modals
   - Subtopics: Past perfect usage, conditional clauses, modal verbs
   - Weightage: High (2 Questions)
   - Solvability in 60 Sec: Easy (30 - 45 seconds)
   - The Rule: Watch out for conditional sentences (e.g., If I had known [past perfect],
     I would have helped [would + have + V3]). TCS tests this pattern constantly.

4. Articles & Nouns
   - Subtopics: Definite/Indefinite articles, uncountable noun rules
   - Weightage: Moderate to High (1-2 Questions)
   - Solvability in 60 Sec: Easy (20 - 35 seconds)
   - The Shortcut: Look out for words like 'advice', 'furniture', or 'luggage' paired
     with plural verbs or articles like 'an advice'. These are instant error giveaways.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
🟨 TIER 2: CONTEXTUAL UNDERSTANDING & CLOZE PATTERNS (High Priority)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
These combine your structural grammar knowledge with your contextual vocabulary skills.

5. Cloze Test
   - Subtopics: Paragraph fill-in-the-blanks, contextual sentence completion
   - Weightage: High (3-5 Questions within 1-2 passages)
   - Solvability in 60 Sec: Moderate (45 - 60 seconds per blank)
   - TCS Internal Insight / Trap to Avoid: Do not fill the blanks one by one. Read the
     entire passage quickly first to establish the tone (positive/negative/neutral)
     before looking at the options.

6. Sentence Completion / Sentence Building
   - Subtopics: Single/Double fillers, vocabulary-based completions
   - Weightage: Moderate to High (2-3 Questions)
   - Solvability in 60 Sec: Easy to Moderate (40 - 55 seconds)
   - The Shortcut: Use the 'positive/negative sign' strategy. If the sentence uses 'although'
     or 'but', the blank must contrast with the rest of the clause.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
🟦 TIER 3: STRUCTURAL LOGIC & SPEED READERS (Moderate Priority)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
These require strong comprehension skills. They can take longer than 60 seconds if
not approached with structural tricks.

7. Sentence Re-arrangement / Para Jumbles
   - Subtopics: Ordering sentences to form a coherent paragraph
   - Weightage: Moderate (2 Questions)
   - Solvability in 60 Sec: Moderate to Hard (55 - 75 seconds)
   - The Strategy: Do not try to guess the perfect order from scratch. Find the mandatory pairs
     (Noun followed by Pronoun, or Cause followed by Effect) and eliminate options.

8. Reading Comprehension
   - Subtopics: Short/Medium passages, central theme, inference-based questions
   - Weightage: Moderate to High (3-4 Questions from 1 passage)
   - Solvability in 60 Sec: Hard (Overall 2-3 minutes for the full set)
   - The Rule: Read the questions before reading the passage. It primes your brain
     to spot the exact keywords and saves you from re-reading the entire block of text.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
⬛ TIER 4: PURE VOCABULARY & STYLE MANIPULATION (Low to Moderate Frequency)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
High risk of time-loss if you overthink. If you know the word, answer it in 15 seconds;
if you do not, eliminate what you can and move on.

9. Synonym & Antonym / Vocabulary
   - Subtopics: Contextual word meanings, direct word tests
   - Weightage: Moderate (1-2 Questions)
   - Solvability in 60 Sec: Easy or Impossible (15 - 30 seconds)
   - The Strategy: Use root words, prefixes, and suffixes to guess the connotation
     if the word is entirely unfamiliar.

10. Spellings
    - Subtopics: Identifying correctly/incorrectly spelled words
    - Weightage: Low (0-1 Question)
    - Solvability in 60 Sec: Easy (20 - 30 seconds)
    - The Trick: Write the word down on your scratchpad before looking at the four
      options. Looking at four variations of a misspelled word can play tricks on your eyes.

11. Formal / Informal Language
    - Subtopics: Tone identification, professional email/text etiquette matching
    - Weightage: Low (0-1 Question)
    - Solvability in 60 Sec: Easy (30 - 50 seconds)
    - The Strategy: Maintain strict corporate neutrality. Look for passive voices
      and polite modal usage ('Would you mind', 'Kindly ensure') for formal options.
