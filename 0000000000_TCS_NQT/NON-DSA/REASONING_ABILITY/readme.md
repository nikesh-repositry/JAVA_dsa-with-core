# TCS NQT Reasoning Ability Exam Blueprint & Topic Priority

**Target Time-per-Question:** 60 Seconds Benchmark

TCS NQT Reasoning Ability is where candidates often lose their momentum. TCS builds complex puzzles and critical reasoning arguments that seem easy but have highly deceptive trap options. Your success relies on identifying high-ROI structural patterns quickly.

**THE CORE STRATEGY:** Treat coding, series, and directions as quick wins (under 45 seconds). Save your cognitive energy for critical reasoning and data arrangement puzzles.

---

### 🟥 TIER 1: HIGH FREQUENCY & SPEED-SOLVING ACCELERATORS (Must Master First)
These topics form the core chunk of the foundational section. If you learn the structural rules and notation tricks, you can consistently crack these in under 45-60 seconds.

1. **Coding Decoding**
   - **Subtopics:** Letter shifting, operational coding, matrix/fictional language coding
   - **Weightage:** High (2-3 Questions)
   - **Solvability in 60 Sec:** Easy (35 - 50 seconds)
   - **The Shortcut:** Never solve by writing the alphabet in real time. Memorize the A-Z forward/reverse positions using the EJOTY (5, 10, 15, 20, 25) framework to spot shifting patterns instantly.

2. **Letter Series & Number Series**
   - **Subtopics:** Missing terms, wrong term in series, alphanumeric combinations
   - **Weightage:** High (2-3 Questions)
   - **Solvability in 60 Sec:** Easy to Moderate (30 - 55 seconds)
   - **TCS Internal Insight / Trap to Avoid:** If the logic doesn't jump out in 30 seconds, don't stare at it. TCS loves hidden double-layer differences (prime numbers, alternating cubes/squares). Calculate the differences first.

3. **Syllogism**
   - **Subtopics:** 2-3 statements with conditions (Some, All, No, Only a few)
   - **Weightage:** High (2 Questions)
   - **Solvability in 60 Sec:** Easy (40 - 50 seconds)
   - **The Shortcut:** Master the standard Venn Diagram approach. Pay extreme attention to "Only a few" and "Possibility" cases, which are standard testing boundaries for Advanced eligibility.

4. **Directions**
   - **Subtopics:** Degree-based turns, shadow problems, shortest distance (Pythagoras theorem)
   - **Weightage:** High (1-2 Questions)
   - **Solvability in 60 Sec:** Easy (40 - 55 seconds)
   - **The Shortcut:** Don't redraw the full map for simple turning questions. Track net degree rotations (Clockwise vs Counter-Clockwise) or net cardinal coordinate movements algebraically.

### 🟨 TIER 2: HIGH WEIGHTAGE CRITICAL & ANALYSIS MODULES (The Digital/Prime Filter)
TCS relies heavily on verbal logic and analytical evaluation. These can be solved quickly if you maintain complete objectivity and avoid personal bias.

5. **Logical Deduction / Critical Reasoning**
   - **Subtopics:** Statements & Assumptions, Conclusions, Arguments, Course of Actions
   - **Weightage:** High (3-4 Questions total)
   - **Solvability in 60 Sec:** Moderate (45 - 65 seconds)
   - **TCS Internal Insight / Trap to Avoid:** The biggest mistake is using external real-world knowledge. Treat the given statement as absolute truth. Assumptions must be implicitly necessary, while conclusions must be 100% logically certain.

6. **Data Sufficiency**
   - **Subtopics:** Math or logic base evaluation using Statement I & Statement II
   - **Weightage:** High (2 Questions)
   - **Solvability in 60 Sec:** Moderate (50 - 70 seconds)
   - **The Shortcut:** Stop calculating the final answer! Your only job is to determine if the data is enough. Check Statement I independently, Statement II independently, and then combine only if both fail.

7. **Blood Relation**
   - **Subtopics:** Coded blood relations (A + B means A is father of B), family tree diagrams
   - **Weightage:** Moderate to High (1-2 Questions)
   - **Solvability in 60 Sec:** Moderate (45 - 60 seconds)
   - **The Shortcut:** For coded blood relations, use Gender Elimination and Generation Gap counting. If the question asks for a male relationship, eliminate options where the person's final symbol forces a female gender.

### 🟦 TIER 3: PATTERN RECOGNITION & VISUAL GEOMETRY (Moderate Priority)
These check spatial perception and raw structural observation.

8. **Visual / Spatial Reasoning & Pattern Recognition**
   - **Subtopics:** Figure completion, embedded figures, next figure in series
   - **Weightage:** Moderate (2 Questions)
   - **Solvability in 60 Sec:** Easy to Moderate (40 - 60 seconds)
   - **The Strategy:** Focus on a single element (like an arrow, dot, or shading) and track its behavior (e.g., 90-degree clockwise rotation).

9. **Cuts & Unfolds & Unboxing a Cube/Dice**
   - **Subtopics:** Paper folding/cutting patterns, opposite faces of open dice
   - **Weightage:** Moderate (1-2 Questions)
   - **Solvability in 60 Sec:** Moderate (45 - 60 seconds)
   - **The Trick:** For dice unboxing, remember that faces separated by exactly one block are always opposites.

10. **Venn Diagrams**
    - **Subtopics:** Logical relationship matching, geometrical region intersections
    - **Weightage:** Moderate (1 Question)
    - **Solvability in 60 Sec:** Easy (30 - 45 seconds)
    - **The Strategy:** Move step-by-step from the outermost classification inward.

---

### ⬛ TIER 4: HIGH-RISK TIME CONSUMERS (Save for Last)

11. **Seating Arrangement & Data Arrangement**
    - **Subtopics:** Linear rows, circular facing inwards/outwards, tabular scheduling
    - **Strategy:** Always construct parallel possibilities simultaneously rather than trying to guess one configuration and restarting.

12. **Decision Making & Attention to Detail**
    - **Subtopics:** Recruitment criteria filters, eligibility condition checking
    - **Strategy:** Create a quick checklist layout on your scratchpad with criteria metrics (Age, Experience, Marks) and check the candidate details systematically.

13. **Clocks and Calendar**
    - **Subtopics:** Date matching, leap year odd days, faulty clock tracking
    - **Strategy:** Stick to modular arithmetic for calendars.
